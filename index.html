Anime explained for Malaysians

We use MyAnimeList (a global anime database) to introduce anime to newcomers in Malaysia - what it is, who watches it, how it's grown, and how to pick a great first show.

Global Map of MyAnimeList users

Even from just one platform - MyAnimeList - millions track what they watch all over the world. We can notice strong communities in the United States, Southeast Asia, and Europe. Japan looks modest here not because anime is unpopular, but because locals often use domestic services (e.g., broadcasters, streaming sites) rather than MAL. Likewise, some countries appear lighter due to internet access, language, or platform preference. Even though this is just from MAL, we can see that, at the very least, Anime is known all around the world.

Who watches anime?

Anime spans genres from action to slice-of-life. On MAL, both Male & Female participation is noted in large quantities, and non-binary/other users are represented too.

This makes sense, since it's mainstream entertainment - more like "TV & film" than a niche subculture. It isn't weird or just for one specific group of people, it's diverse, with something for everyone.

Tip: Total Counts shown in the center; filtered participants from MAL.

Formats & Genres

"Anime" isn't one thing, its a format or style: like LiveAction. TV series dominate, but there are movies, OVAs, ONAs, specials, and music videos.

Pair that with genres - from Comedy and Romance to Mecha, Mystery, and more - and you get something for any and everyone, paired with any mood. It's not just childrens shows; in fact, a good portion is catered to teens and adults, filled with mature themes ranging from war to mental illness and even mild, yet popular themes like romance and adventure.

Use the dropdown to explore genres within a format.

Growth over the years

Anime as a type of film has had production scaled dramatically after the 1990s. Seasonal TV output climbs, ONAs rise with streaming, and films/movies stay steady. As the years go by, and as Anime becomes more popular and mainsteam, more and more shows release with different themes, genres and stories. There is constant freshness for old viewers, and options for new comers. In any case, this format will continue to grow at a steady pace.

Each layer counts titles per year within that format.

What to pick: Critically acclaimed or Most popular?

From the scatterplot, its easy to see that in general - shows with higher ratings are often more popular. There are exceptions to this rule ofcourse, but generally, a healthy popularity and quality plus word-of-mouth helps beginners land a hit.

Its nice to pick something that is popular and highly rated, so you can enjoy it and have plenty of people to talk about it with.
